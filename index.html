Reaper: Start Up Guide

Installing Reaper On PC

This section will provide instructions to install Reaper on your PC. To install Reaper:

  1. Go to the Reaper download page and download the latest version of Reaper.
  2. Open the folder you have chosen to download Reaper to.
  3. Double click on the Reaper install file (reaper_Version#_install.exe).
  4. Click I Agree after having read the License Agreement.
  1. Choose installation directory and click Next.
  1. Choose Install once desired components have been selected (Default selection is typically correct).
  1. Click Close once installation is complete.

Creating Your First Reaper Project

This section will get you started with your first Reaper project. These instructions will guide you in making a simple voice recording, and then editing that recording.

Setting reaper up for recording and editing

To set up reaper for recording and editing:

  1. Open Reaper by double clicking the Reaper icon on your desktop, or finding it in your program files.
  2. Click ok on the opening screen.
  3. On the Reaper screen, move your cursor up to File and select New Project.
  1. Left Click in the top right corner of the screen on Audio Device Settings (it may read Audio Device Closed)
  1. Confirm the selection under Audio System is the audio system to be used with Reaper.
Note: If you do not have an external audio interface installed, set this to your default sound card and Windows sound driver (Direct Sound).
  1. Confirm Input Device is set to default microphone (your PC’s mic).
  1. Confirm Output Device is set to default output device (your PC’s speaker/headphone jack).
  1. Click OK to apply the new settings.
  1. Move and left click on Track menu at the top of the main Reaper screen.
  1. Select Insert New Track
  1. Left Click the Monitor icon twice to disable it. (Record Monitoring Off)
  1. Left click the Track Record icon.(Record Armed)

The monitor icons should now be moving in correspondence to your PC’s microphone picking up sounds. Reaper is now configured to start your first project.

REcording your first project

This section will guide you through making your very first Reaper project. The steps are designed to be simple and clear. Once you have recorded and saved the first version of your project, feel free to experiment with different effects and settings.

Note: Reaper features an unlimited Undo feature, which combined with sequential and prudent saving practices will always allow to revert back to the original settings.

While we won’t be able to explore all of the advanced features in this guide, there are a multitude of resources online that cover the more advanced features of Reaper.

A pair of headphones with a regular 3.5mm jack is strongly suggested for recording and editing.

To start a new project:

  1. Connect your headphones to the audio jack out of your PC.
  1. Using the track you created earlier , click on the monitor icon to ensure track monitoring is enabled. (Click here if you need help setting up a new track.)
  1. Arm the track for recording. (Click here if you need help identifying the track record button.)You should now hear the microphone through your headphones.
  1. Press the red Record button at the bottom center of the screen when you are ready to start.

Note: Record yourself counting to five slowly, breaking slightly between each number.

Congratulations, you’ve just recorded your first Reaper sound. Notice how the wave forms on your screen differ in size and intensity. The peaks represent the loudest part of the sounds (in this case the numbers you recorded), while the low portions represent the quieter parts of the sound.

Editing your project

Now that you have created your sound, the video below will teach you to edit it.

First you will add an effect called compression. Compression takes a sound, and rids it of the highest and lowest parts of the wave form. It is used to keep sounds more uniform and professional sounding.

Second, you will learn how to add another effect called reverb to the sound. Reverb, adds an echo to the sound wave, similar to recording a sound in an enclosed space like a cave.

Finally, you will learn to cut the sound into pieces and rearrange the order of the number’s sequence.
